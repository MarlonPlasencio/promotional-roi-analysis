# Promotional Campaign ROI Analysis

**Tools:** Python, pandas, matplotlib, seaborn  
**Data Source:** Superstore Sales Dataset (Kaggle — publicly available)  
**Records:** 9,994 orders | $2.3M in sales | 2014–2017

---

## About This Project

Personal portfolio project analyzing the relationship between discount 
strategy and profitability across 9,994 retail orders. Built using Python 
in a Jupyter notebook with narrative analysis cells explaining the business 
implications of each finding.

---

## Key Findings

- **Aggressive discounting (31%+) destroyed $125,007 in profit** while 
  generating only $260K in sales — a -48.2% margin
- **No-discount orders earned a 29.5% margin**, generating $321K profit 
  on $1.09M in sales
- **Office Supplies** is the most damaged category under aggressive 
  discounting, reaching -119% margin
- **Discounts above 20% produce negative margins across all 3 categories** 
  — Furniture, Office Supplies, and Technology

---

## Business Recommendations

1. Cap discounts at 20% company-wide
2. Eliminate aggressive discounting in Furniture entirely
3. Grow no-discount sales channels through loyalty and bundling strategies
4. Audit the 1,166 aggressive discount orders to identify root cause
